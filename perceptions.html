Perception is Everything

Why aren't women self-confident?

Women's markedly lower self-confidence appears in childhood. Starting at a young age, other peoples' perceptions of female students plays a role in their self-confidence, exacerbating the STEM gap. A study found that girls are only perceived as competent as boys when the girls are seen as "working harder, behaving better, and being more eager to learn"-- despite performing just as well academically.6
